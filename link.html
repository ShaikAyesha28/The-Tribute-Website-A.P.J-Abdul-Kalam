A.P.J Abdul Kalam

1931 - 2015

Missile Man of India

A.P.J. Abdul Kalam as the “Missile Man of India,” was one of the most inspiring leaders our countryhas ever seen. Born on 15 October 1931 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, he came from a humble family but rose to become one ofIndia’s greatest scientists.He played a key role in India’s missile development, space research, and nuclear programs.His contributions to ISRO and DRDO brought global recognition to India. Dr. Kalam was the chief architect of thePokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998.In 2002, he became the 11th President of India, remembered as the “People’s President” because of his simplicity, kindness, and love for students. He always believed that young minds can change the future ofthe nation.He wrote many motivational books such as Wings of Fire, Ignited Minds, and India 2020, which continue toinspire millions.Dr. Kalam passed away on 27 July 2015, while doing what he loved the most—teaching students. His life teaches us to dream big, work hard, stay humble, and never give up.
